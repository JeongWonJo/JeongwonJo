Easing Long-haul Uncertainty during the COVID-19

Information Sharing in Local Community Facebook Group to Ease Uncertainty during the COVID-19

Project Overview

📌 Objectives

  • Understanding how people use online local community groups to ease uncertainty at the early phase of the pandemic
  • Ideating design recommendations that can support disaster information sharing in online local community groups

🔎  Methods

  • Content Analysis using constant comparative analysis

🙌  My Role

  • Principal investigator
    • Collaborated with two other researchers
    • Collected and thematically analyzed 256 information-seeking Facebook posts
    • Ideated design recommendations
    • Wrote and published an academic paper in iConference 2021

🤜 Research Motives

  1. Surging uncertainty at the initial phase of the pandemic, due to new policies and safety measurers people don't have experience with
  2. Up-to-date and accurate situational information can be retrieved from local community-focused social media
  3. Information people seeking during an epidemic crisis is unknown

‍

🧐 Research Questions

What types of local-specific information were sought in local communities at the initial phase of COVID-19?

How can social media focused on local communities can be better designed to support information sharing for an epidemic crisis?

🔎 Method

1. Finding Local Community-focused Facebook Group

Criteria for Choosing the Facebook Group for Analysis:
• Covers a local area with consistently high severity of COVID-19 cases
• Open to anyone to join, view, and post
• High group activity (e.g., average posts per day, the number of members)

→ “Local Community Support for COVID-19 Relief in Austin, Texas” Facebook Group

‍

2. Analyzing Information Seeking Posts

Selecting Posts for Analysis:
• Posts posted between March 14 to October 25 in 2020
• Posts with phrases like “Does anyone have suggestions...”,“Does anyone know...”, “Where/how can I...”, “How do you...”, “Has anyone...”, “Looking for recommendations/advice/suggestions...”
• Posts irrelevant to seeking local-specific information were filtered out
→ 256 information seeking posts

Content Analysis: Used the constant comparative analysis approach to code posts based on the type of information the poster was asking;
Discarded codes that are not local-specific or relevant to information that could be obtained from official national agencies

2. Uncertainty about Local COVID-19 Regulations

Unfamiliar with emerging, frequently changing regulations that differ by county

Poster: Will someone please share the official guidance for Texas on high-risk people? My 83-year old mother with asthma decided it was a good idea to go to church this weekend.
Commenter: High risk people should not be in groups greater than 10
Poster: She is in El Paso.
Commenter: Ok, it appears to be better there than here. They are at the lowest level...and churches are 50% capacity.

‍

3. Seeking Confirmation on Social Behaviors

Pandemic forced people to question what behaviors were 'reasonable'
→ Reasonable behaviors are socially defined, so asked how locals behaved
→ Diverse opinions did not reach a consensus, but they were all rational

Poster: Am I unreasonable by turning away clients who work with many other people?...by accepting public-facing clients, I’d be exponentially increasing the risk of my salon becoming a vector for covid.
Commenter 1: It is your right to screen in anyway you choose. It is ultimately your health, the health of your clients and your liability
Commenter 2: Are you saying you won’t do all the people on
the front line? Like grocery workers?...There’s a fine line for some form of discrimination here.

Design Recommendations

Based on the findings on types of local-specific information sought in local-focused online communities, I suggest design implications that can better online local epidemic relief community for uncertainty management.

1. Hyperlocal and Hypertemporal Information Requests

🤲 User Information Needs
🧠 Design Recommendation
Recommendations of local stores that take proper preventive measures
Users can share a map of local stores with checkboxes for safety protocols
e.g., sanitized carts or limits the number of customers
Less crowded local place
Crowdsourced geo-tagging feature e.g.,crowdedness by red, orange, and green; inventory tags or photos of shelves at stores with timestamps for hard-to-find items
Up-to-date busy hours at stores
Inventory status at local stores or where to get a specific item

2. Confusing COVID-19 Regulations

🤲 User Information Needs
🧠 Design Recommendation
Regulations differing by county is confusing
Setting up online disaster relief groups on a county basis
Frequently changing, emerging regulations are confusing
A banner to specify a phase of regulation; the level of policy strictness and correct policies can be disseminated under control.

3. Collective Sense-making Under Uncertainty

Collective sense-making wasn’t always smooth with conflicting opinions
→ Logical on both sides but did not completely resolve uncertainties
→ Can lead to rejecting conflicting opinions + halting their assessments
→ Can pose a risk to one’s health amidst an epidemic crisis
→ Nudge people to hold a dialogue with others and outline different viewpoints

A Venue for Public Deliberation Systems with a Pro and a Con List
who agree with the poster on the pro list, who disagree on the con list
• make the collective sense-making process more visible and structured
• the poster conveniently weigh confirmations and denials

🌟 Future Research Insights

Future studies can observe other communities based on different areas or analyze posts uploaded in a different timeframe of the pandemic to see if other information demands exist
