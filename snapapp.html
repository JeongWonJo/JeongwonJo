User Experiences and Missed Design Opportunities in Mobile Applications for Public Food Aid

Project Overview

📌 Objectives

  • Evaluating mobile apps for Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) from the end-users (beneficiaries) standpoint
  • Identifying what end-user values are not supported on SNAP apps
  • Ideating design recommendations that support end-user values for SNAP apps

🔎  Methods

  • Value-sensitive Design
    • Conceptual Investigation: Literature Review
    • Empirical and Technical Investigation:
      Thematic Analysis of mobile app reviews and Reddit posts

🙌  My Role

  • Principal investigator
    • Collaborated with two other researchers
    • Designed methods
    • Collected data and led data analysis
    • Ideated design recommendations
    • Wrote an academic paper

Value-Sensitive Design

Value-Sensitive Design (VSD) is a methodological approach used in technology design, which accounts for human values throughout the design process.

Conceptual Investigation: understanding and articulating various values, stakeholder groups, and value conflicts that are inherent in any given design scenario.

Empirical Investigation: assessing the human experience, expectations, and interactions with technology

Technical Investigation: understanding how technology might either support or conflict with human values and finding solutions to ensure that these conflicts are minimized

e.g.) user values in AI: accountability, transparency, interpretability

Conceptual Investigation

RQ: What public values should be ideally supported for SNAP mobile apps?

🔎 Method

I performed secondary research by reviewing prior works on the public value-based digital government system

📝 Findings

I identified four types of public values that government infrastructure should ideally support: quality of information and service, organizational efficiency, openness, and responsiveness

Values
Description
Quality of Information & Service
Quality of information can be evaluated based on its accuracy, timeliness, relevancy, understandability, and level of detail. Quality of service is measured with transactional functionalities that enable, e.g., virtually downloading, completing, and submitting application forms or accessing archival data
Organizational Efficiency
It can be achieved with cutting processing time and costs, e.g., shared public information, IT enabled contact points, and well-trained public staff
Openness
The transparency and accountability of government institutions, which includes the disclosure of budget and expenses, the availability of public policy documents, the disclosure of decision making processes, and the ability of citizens to make complaints
Responsiveness
The extent to which a public organization interact with citizens that can be measured with online case tracking, ability to make inquiries, timely responses to inquiries

Empirical & Technical Investigation

RQ1: How public values for digital government are supported or violated in existing mobile apps for SNAP management?

RQ2: Which of the values of SNAP beneficiaries are missing in the existing mobile apps for SNAP management?

🔎 Method

Data Collection of app reviews: Searched a keyword SNAP, food stamps, EBT (Electronic Balance Transfer) in the Android and the iOS app stores

Criteria for selecting app stores: (1) app store listing is in English; (2) app allows SNAP benefit management (discerned by reading their app store descriptions); (3) app is available in both Android and Apple app stores

Sorted reviews with the app store filter and extracted top-50 "most helpful reviews'' from each app store using the Python package app_store_scraper.
For apps fewer than 50 reviews, all reviews were sampled.
573 Android app reviews with average 57 words per review (SD=32)
523 Apple app reviews with average 79 words per review (SD=52)

Data Collection of r/foodstamps Reddit posts: Web scrawling to collect the top 3000 posts, ranked according to the number of votes each received; Filtered out deleted posts, resulting in 2966 posts

Data Analysis: thematic analysis

  1. Individually developed open codes with another research member
    The focus for app reviews: identifying the features in current apps;
    The focus for Reddit posts: identifying features that could potentially address an issue raised in each post
  2. Collectively created a codebook
    Created a codebook, comprising code names, definitions, and exemplary quotes, with each code symbolizing an app feature that appeared consistently across multiple app reviews and posts.
  3. Mapped the codes with our pre-established conceptual framework of four public values in ideal government systems
  4. Performed another round of data coding

👀 Findings

Values 1: Quality of Information & Service

Balance update and management

Feature 1: an automatic refreshment of the EBT balance

  • improved the efficiency and convenience of checking EBT balances
  • reduced embarrassment of not having enough balance at a register
"when I'm shopping I could just open the app and be reassured of my balance so I don’t get that 'holding up of the line, or awkward stare for it not going through'"      - EE A7

Feature 2: the date and amount of the next benefit deposit, expiration dates of deposited benefits

  • enhance budgeting skills and prevent early benefit depletion
"how do I find out if I'll receive it this month and on what day?...Food is starting to get a little tight so I wanted to make sure I planned ahead."      - Reddit 211

Feature 3: transaction histories with expenditure amounts and locations

  • transaction histories with expenditure amounts and locations allow cross-verification of EBT balances and monitor discrepancies
"the app [could] include SNAP purchase transactions, in order to verify accuracy of balance shown"      - NAH A22

EBT eligible items/stores

  • locating EBT-friendly retailers reduces embarrassment from discovering retailers or some items in a cart are not SNAP-eligible and having to abandon a purchase
"I've been freaking myself out about going to the store...I worry it'll look obvious I’m worried about affording stuff...and horrified of having to put groceries back at the register because I can't afford them. Is there some kind of list I can look at so I can make a plan before I go?"     - Reddit 204
  • creating a shopping list in advance reduces embarrassment from not having sufficient balance at a register
"I could make my [shopping] list based on ad prices or prices I knew well enough to estimate. I knew before I got to the store if I was within my budget"     - Prov G40

‍

Values 2: Organizational Efficiency

Reduced Need for In-person Visits
  • saves time and money, emphasizing benefits of continuously digitizing SNAP benefit-related processes
  • reduces humiliation they face from long lines and attitudes of caseworkers at government offices
"sincerely "Thanks" you for...helping us maintain our dignity by providing accessible information online"     - YTB A14

Online Document Submission

  • no more delays or missed notifications from communication with the government via mail
  • occasionally resulted in inadvertent benefit cancellations or denials from not valid document formats or missing documents
"The pictures may be clear on your phone but the caseworkers will not get a clear picture on their end, so they will deny your case, saying you didn't turn in your documents or that they were too blurry. It happened to me 7 times already"      - YTB A43
  • caseworkers failing to locate app-submitted documents
"even though it showed completed and sent...they claim not to get it on their end which in the ends denies benefits"      - YTB A25

‍

Values 3: Openness

Explanation for EBT denial or amount
  • no apps provided rationale for their ineligibility or changed benefit amounts, or they were too technical, unclear, or lacked details
"I keep getting denied and they won't give me a reason...I could really use advice on what to do or what other resources I have to get food."      - Reddit 75
"They have me listed as ineligible and the reason listed is TECHNICAL_ELIGIBILITY IN_SANCTION_PERIOD. Does anyone know [what] this means?"      - Reddit 295
Filing an Appeal or Complaints
  • no apps provided a feature to file an appeal or complaints, leading to long waiting period
"we got denied for "Too much income"...I've tried...calling multiple times...just to get the auto reply...to call back later"    - Reddit 2719
  • ‍beneficiaries did not get assistance in filing an appeal from their caseworkers, who at times explicitly discouraged such attempts
"yelled at me and told me that what I was asking for would be fraud…She said I could ask for a hearing but the ruling wouldn’t be in my favor"    - Reddit 71
  • beneficiaries were reluctant to file complaints against caseworkers, due to fear of potential retaliation
"if I request a new caseworker, would they make a note of it somewhere so that people could potentially discriminate against me in that way?"    - Reddit 140

‍

Values 4: Responsiveness

Interview/renewal notification
  • untimely app notifications for document renewal submissions or upcoming interviews led to benefit denial or cancellation
"the app doesn't notify you...until a few days later and by that time your case will get rejected. They need to update this app and actually put some care into this. [This] is people food"    - NAH A7
  • caseworkers failed to call beneficiaries on the interview scheduled date; despite repeated attempts to reach caseworkers via the designated number, they received no response, resulting in benefit denial
"I was sent a letter to...have my phone interview...I called and called and called and left several messages...and no one got back to me...Now, they've denied me, saying I didn't complete my phone interview and that it was my responsibility"    - R2
App support
  • negative experiences with the app support service or dissatisfaction with no app support available at all, leading to benefit interruptions
  • ill-equipped government employees to offer satisfactory technical assistance
"it has no option to speak to a representative about this app..my county assistance...are just as lost as me"    - CE A1
  • Lack of app support led users to feel neglected and marginalize, indicating the ethical implications of effective technical support:
"Too bad the developers...have apparently abandoned the app and those that depended on it"    - EE A25

Design Recommendations

Through our analysis, we identified that dignity, the concept that incorporates self-image in relation to treatment from others and individual agency, is underrepresented in current SNAP mobile apps.

Designing a mobile app for social safety nets and its impact can be more complex than apps for commercial purposes since the dignity encounters of its users are more likely to result in violations due to their being in marginalized and vulnerable positions in society.

Goal 1. Enhancing Self-Esteem of SNAP Beneficiaries

☹️ User Pain Points
🧠 Design Recommendation
Public food aid beneficiaries often face embarrassment and humiliation, leading to internalized poverty stigma
• An anonymous online forum that encourages storytelling can be integrated; help beneficiaries recognize and legitimize unjust stigmatizing practices with others
Not sufficient app support is provided
• A chatbot service for app support can be integrated, while designers should consider the potential impacts of word choices on the beneficiaries' identities and choose empowering language
Waiting in long lines at government and delayed service can give users a feeling that they are positioned in lower social status
• a real-time congestion updates
• estimated waiting times for different time slots throughout the day

Goal 2. Enhancing Agency of SNAP Beneficiaries

☹️ User Pain Points
🧠 Design Recommendation
Unfair treatment from caseworkers but:
• fear of potential retaliation as a result of filing complaints
•no app feature to file complaints
• Record and monitor interactions with caseworkers via the app
•Identify unfair treatment from caseworkers using automated natural language processing
• The complaint filing feature, assuring usersthat unjust retaliation is unlikely
Erroneous benefit denials
• A feature for beneficiaries to directly correct their information
Beneficiaries missing benefit interview calls from caseworkers or caseworkers failing to call beneficiaries
• An app notification for interview scheduled dates
• Document interview communication attempts from both parties to reduce the risk of beneficiaries unfairly losing benefits
Did not understand why they were asked to provide sensitive personal information and felt humiliated
• Explanations of why each information is asked on the online document submission form
How government decisions about benefit denials or benefit amounts were made was, to beneficiaries, a black box
• Explanations of how government decisions were made with lay language
• Guidance for future action towards potentially different outcomes
• The appeal filing feature

🌟 Future Research Insights

Designs for Filing an Appeal or Complaints

Designs may need to be tailored depending on factors like:
• whether appeals are reviewed by a human or an algorithm
• the type of appeal (e.g., an appeal to revert an already-made decision based on the original information, to contest an application denial, or to dispute changed allocated amounts)
• personal characteristics (e.g., an individual's familiarity with benefit programs)

Further Design Studies

• Interviews to explore app usability
• Participatory design studies to directly embed end-user values into designs
